IV: Sentiment Analysis in Viva Aerobus survey

June 12, 2022

Viva Aerobus is a well-known airline that has established itself as a leading low-cost carrier in the aviation industry. With a focus on affordability and accessibility, Viva Aerobus strives to provide passengers with cost-effective travel options without compromising on quality and safety. By adopting efficient operational strategies, such as optimizing routes and implementing innovative cost-saving measures, Viva Aerobus has succeeded in offering competitive fares to a wide range of destinations. Additionally, the airline wants to improve the customer experience through superior service and hassle-free travel.

In order to address the need for improvement and better understand the sentiments of passengers, surveys play a crucial role in gathering valuable feedback. Recognizing the significance of customer input, I conducted a comprehensive sentiment analysis on the comments received. This analysis allowed me to understand the underlying emotions and opinions expressed by the respondents. By carefully examining the feedback, I gained insights into areas that require enhancement and identified specific aspects that customers appreciate. Armed with this valuable information, I was able to create strategic plans and make informed decisions to further improve the overall experience offered by Viva Aerobus.

The first step was to clean the database by removing various elements that could potentially interfere with the sentiment analysis. This involved eliminating exclamation marks, periods, commas, one letter inputs, emojis, flags, and other similar symbols. By doing this, we ensured that the analysis would be conducted on a more accurate foundation. This step aimed to eliminate any potential noise or bias that could arise from these characters, allowing for a more precise and reliable evaluation of the sentiments expressed in the comments received.

Next, a word cloud is printed in order to understand the most popular words. This visual representation provides a comprehensive overview, highlighting the popularity of specific terms within the dataset. It is important when identifying key themes and areas of focus, allowing for more targeted improvements and enhancements to be implemented based on the findings. After understanding the data, since the goal is to predict if a comment will be positive or negative, a logistic regression algorithm was applied. Logistic regression is a statistical modeling technique used to predict the probability of a binary outcome, and it is particularly useful when the dependent variable is categorical, with two possible outcomes, such as “yes” or “no”, or “0” or “1.” The goal of logistic regression is to estimate the probability of the occurrence of a specific outcome by fitting the data to a logistic function or sigmoid curve. Unlike linear regression, which predicts continuous outcomes, logistic regression focuses on estimating the likelihood of belonging to a particular category.

In conclusion, the initial analysis of the graphs revealed that users encounter the most challenges with payment methods, prices, and seating arrangements. This valuable information serves as a foundation for addressing the issues identified. By understanding the specific pain points expressed by users, efforts can be directed towards improving these areas to enhance customer satisfaction and overall experience.

Moreover, the model created through this analysis acts as a reliable tool for ongoing monitoring and detection of potential problems. With regular updates and daily utilization, any emerging issues, such as the rejection of a particular credit card, can be quickly identified. This approach enables prompt actions to be taken, ensuring that necessary adjustments and fixes are implemented promptly. By leveraging the insights gained from the model, Viva Aerobus can maintain a proactive stance, continuously improving its services and addressing any concerns that arise.

Next steps include:

  1. Feedback Categorization: Develop a systematic categorization framework to classify customer comments based on themes, such as pricing, customer service, or in-flight experience. This will provide a structured approach to analyzing and addressing specific areas of improvement.

  2. Actionable Insights: Identify key findings from the analysis and translate them into actionable insights. Prioritize areas for improvement based on the frequency and severity of customer concerns, ensuring that efforts are focused on addressing high-impact issues.

  3. Qualitative Analysis: Supplement the quantitative analysis with qualitative methods, such as focus groups or in-depth interviews, to gather detailed feedback and better understand the underlying reasons behind customers’ concerns and preferences.
